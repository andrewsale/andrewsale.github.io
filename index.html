Andrew Sale

Short bio:

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. My research is in the area of Geometric Group Theory. I have taken personal leave from my position to move back to the mainland, and now live in North Carolina where I am learning some new skills.

I completed my PhD at the University of Oxford in 2012 (where a PhD is called a DPhil), and then went on to have postdoc positions at Cornell Univeristy, University of Rennes 1, and Vanderbilt University. After securing a tenure-track position at the University of Hawaii, I was awarded a Simons Collaboration Grant to support my research.
